Digital Futures

Get into London's booming tech world


I'm a student I'm a business

What is Digital Futures?

Digital Futures is a full-time creative coding bootcamp taking place from 4th–31st July.

Is this for me?

If you are Lambeth resident aged 18–30 who wants to upgrade your digital skills, this course is for you.

Apply now

What will we do?

We will will design and build an app for the interactive dance production 'David'. The app will be used in a production at the Strive Festival at the Southbank Centre in August.

What topics will be covered?

The entire curriculum can be described as building the app. Students will learn by doing the following:

In English

  • How to design apps focused on user needs
  • Learning how to quickly build and test digital tools
  • Design principles
  • Digital graphic design focused on users
  • Problem-solving skills
  • Coding skills for websites and apps
  • Team work and collaboration skills
  • Using data on the web
  • Making things work for those with disabilities
  • How to protect an application from hackers

In Tech Speak

  • User Centred design process
  • Rapid prototyping
  • Design thinking
  • User Experience (UX) design
  • Computational thinking
  • Coding in HTML, CSS & JavaScript
  • GitHub and collaborative coding
  • Web Services (HTTP & APIs)
  • Accessibility
  • Cybersecurity

What is David?

David is an interactive contemporary dance production.

For more information about David, please visit Pell Ensemble.

Watch the video below

What else is covered?

Each student will work with a dedicated mentor from the local community who will provide guidance and support throughout the course.

Key employability skills will be covered by a series of workshops, and local employers will come in to share insights and advice about their industry.

What happens after the course?

We help you apply for a work placement to put your skills into practice.

Your mentor will provide you with career support for at least two months after the course.

Where can your new skills take you?

We expect our graduates to be able to perform any of the following roles:

In English

  • A designer who specialises in websites
  • A designer who focuses on user experience
  • A developer who works with web technologies
  • A developer who works with servers and databases
  • A developer who builds user-facing apps
  • A developer who is comfortable on the server and in the client
  • A technician who looks after IT systems

In Tech Speak

  • Web Designer
  • User Experience Designer
  • Web Developer
  • Back-end developer
  • Front-end developer
  • Full-stack developer
  • Systems Administrator
  • Project Manager
  • Product Manager

Who is leading the course?

You will be spending the month with Creative Developer Ali Blackwell

In 2011 he co-founded Decoded with the goal of teaching the world to code.

He is an award-winning web designer and developer, and an enthusiastic teacher.

To find out more, check out Ali on LinkedIn and Twitter – or Google him!

This all sounds great, what else do I need to know?

  • The course runs from 10am to 5pm each day
  • Lunch and travel expenses are covered for the entire programme
  • The course will take place at Short St, London SE1 8LJ
